Job Board

Mission & Vision

The Governance Lab at NYU Tandon School of Engineering aims to improve our lives and communities by changing how we govern using new technology. We design, implement and study high impact, multi-disciplinary projects with partners in city, state, and national governments and other public interest organizations in the United States and around the world.

Current Openings

GovLab Fellow or GovLab Senior Fellow

GovLab Fellow or GovLab Senior Fellow

The GovLab works with public institutions to help them govern more legitimately and effectively. (More info at www.thegovlab.org)

We focus on how to bring new technology, methods and scientific insights to bear to open up and improve how institutions make decisions and solve problems at the local, state, national and international level.

We are seeking a new GovLab Fellow -- and open to receiving applications from senior or junior applicants -- to assist/lead projects, conduct research and launch experiments in collaboration with our public partners, design and run training programs, write and publish about GovLab’s work on governance innovation, develop new projects.

Our Fellows are changemakers, passionate about mission driven work who know how to leverage innovation, technology and data to solve public problems. You have a proven track record for getting things done.

As a Fellow, you may be engaged in work on CrowdLaw to promote democratic engagement in lawmaking. You may participate in a Smarter Crowdsourcing project to accelerate policy advising to leading public institutions or help to scale our extensive work on innovation skills training.

Depending on your background and level of experience, your work may include:

  • Executing on existing projects as part of a multidisciplinary team
  • Designing and planning projects and research
  • Designing crowdsourcing and expert sourcing initiatives to solve problems
  • Identifying and capturing new ideas and practices on how to make decisions and solve public problems;
  • Curating and reaching out to top experts
  • Working with partners to establish collaborations
  • Drafting grant proposals
  • Running online engagements and convenings with experts and the public
  • Conducting interviews
  • Writing case studies
  • Writing policy briefs and implementation memos for policymakers and/or foundations.
  • Conducting rapid research reviews
  • Publishing blog posts, articles and social media on governance innovation topics
  • Writing project proposals
  • Developing new projects with partners/business development
  • Teaching, training and coaching would-be public problem solvers

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Strong project management skills
  • Proven research and analytical skills
  • Passion for the public interest
  • Strong writing and editing abilities
  • Ability to help run events and convenings
  • Familiarity with new research tools and interest in new ways of presenting information
  • Attention to detail in your writing and communication
  • Ability to work online independently and in collaboration with others
  • The flexibility necessary to thrive in a “permanent startup” environment
  • Business development skills
  • Graduate degree in technology, computer sciences, communications, a social science, social work or public service, public policy or administration, international affairs, law, business administration or economics or comparable professional experience
  • Expertise in education or health policy a strong plus

We are committed to:

  • Serving the public interest
  • Working collaboratively and in partnership with others
  • Respecting the expertise and experience of individuals and communities
  • Using innovation, technology and data responsibly and ethically
  • Using evidence of what works from research and from the field to inform what we do.
  • Engaging with diverse communities and individuals to improve the legitimacy of our efforts.
  • Undertaking work that has the potential to improve lives, build communities and strengthen democracy
  • Fair, equitable and professional working practices.

We are based in Brooklyn, New York but you may live anywhere and work remotely.

Occasionally, our Senior Fellows are part-time (ie. they also teach part-time). We are open to flexible working arrangements on GovLab projects with public partners.

GovLab is an equal opportunity employer that embraces diversity and is committed to building a team that represents a variety of backgrounds, perspectives, and skills. We are committed to providing employees with a work environment free of discrimination and harassment.

To Apply:

  • Interested applicants should apply to fdejohn@thegovlab.org. Please share a CV, cover letter and/or portfolio with description of past projects, writing samples (memos, articles, op-eds, blogs and/or academic writing).
  • Expected start date: As soon as feasible for all parties involved.
